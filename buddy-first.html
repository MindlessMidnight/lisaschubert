From matching to meaningful connections

Many platforms help people find others. Few help them actually meet. This project explores how reducing uncertainty and lowering the first step can turn matches into real connections.

Why do connections fail?

I started by exploring how people connect through shared hobbies. Research revealed a different problem. The challenge is not finding people. It is taking the first step.

Meaningful connections often fail because the step from match to action is too big, unclear, or emotionally risky.

Key insights

The first step is the real barrier

Users do not struggle to find people. They struggle to act. Uncertainty leads to inaction.

Small steps drive behaviour

People are more likely to act when actions are simple and clearly defined. Large steps create friction.

Emotional safety comes first

Users need to feel safe before reaching out. Warmth and similarity influence decisions more than competence.

Clarity beats choice

Too many options lead to decision avoidance. Users prefer a few clear next steps.

These insights show that the core challenge is not matching users. The challenge is enabling action.

Design decision

People do not change behaviour because they are encouraged to. They change when the situation makes action easier. Instead of motivating users, the solution reduces the barrier to act.

Solution concept

Option A

An assistant encourages users to attend events alone. This approach focuses on changing mindset.

Option B: Buddy First

Users are matched before attending. This removes the uncertainty of arriving alone and changes the situation itself.

Wireframes

The core flow focuses on one transition. Find a person. Start contact. Meet together.

Usability testing

I tested the concept with users to understand whether profiles feel approachable and whether the transition from match to meeting is clear.
